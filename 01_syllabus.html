Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to horticulture, covering plant science, soil management, propagation techniques, pest and disease identification, and sustainable growing practices. Students will develop both theoretical knowledge and practical skills through classroom instruction and hands-on greenhouse labs.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Identify and describe the major structures and functions of flowering plants.
  2. Classify soils by texture and composition and recommend amendments for horticultural use.
  3. Demonstrate seed germination, cuttings, and division propagation techniques.
  4. Identify common garden pests and diseases and select appropriate Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies.
  5. Apply sustainable and environmentally responsible growing practices.
  6. Plan and maintain a productive planting schedule across seasons.

Late Work Policy

Lab reports submitted within 24 hours of the deadline receive a 10% deduction. Submissions 24–72 hours late receive a 25% deduction. No lab reports will be accepted more than 72 hours after the deadline without a documented medical or family emergency approved in advance by the instructor.

Quizzes cannot be made up except in cases of documented medical emergencies notified before the quiz date. The final project deadline is firm — no extensions will be granted.

Greenhouse Lab Policy

Students must wear closed-toe shoes and gardening gloves during all greenhouse lab sessions. Lab sessions run Thursdays 10:00–11:20 AM in the campus greenhouse (Building C). Attendance at all six lab sessions is mandatory. Missing more than two lab sessions without documented reason will result in a grade of zero for the lab report component.

Academic Integrity

All submitted work must be your own. Collaboration on lab reports is permitted for data collection only — written analysis must be completed independently. Use of AI-generated text without attribution is considered academic dishonesty under NBCC Policy 4.2.1. Students may use AI tools as a learning aid but must declare this in a brief note at the top of any submission.
